§ 1 Introduction

1.1

Spendly ("we," "us," or "our") operates the personal expense tracking application available at spendly.app and any related services (collectively, the "Service"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your personal information when you use the Service.

1.2

We are committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal data responsibly, transparently, and in accordance with applicable data protection laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the Information Technology Act 2000 (India), and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) where applicable.

1.3

By accessing or using the Service, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with any part of this policy, you should discontinue use of the Service immediately.

1.4

This policy applies to all users of the Service regardless of the device or method used to access it. It does not apply to third-party websites, applications, or services that may be linked to or from the Service, each of which has its own privacy practices.

1.5

Questions, comments, and requests regarding this Privacy Policy are welcomed and should be directed to our designated contact address. Details are provided in Section 17 of this document.

§ 2 Definitions

2.1

"Service" refers to the Spendly web application, including all features, functionality, content, and user interfaces made available through spendly.app.

2.2

"Personal Data" means any information that relates to an identified or identifiable natural person. An identifiable person is one who can be identified, directly or indirectly, by reference to an identifier such as a name, identification number, location data, or online identifier.

2.3

"Processing" means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, including collection, recording, organisation, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, or erasure.

2.4

"Data Controller" means the natural or legal person who determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data. For the purposes of this policy, Spendly acts as the Data Controller with respect to your personal information.

2.5

"Data Processor" means a natural or legal person that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Data Controller. Certain third-party service providers engaged by us act as Data Processors.

2.6

"Consent" means any freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous indication of the data subject's wishes by which they signify agreement to the processing of Personal Data relating to them. Consent may be withdrawn at any time without detriment.

§ 3 Information We Collect

3.1

Account Registration Data. When you create an account, we collect your full name, email address, and a hashed version of your chosen password. We never store passwords in plain text.

3.2

Profile Information. You may optionally provide additional profile details such as a preferred currency, budget limits, or a profile display name. This information is stored solely to personalise your experience.

3.3

Financial Data You Enter. The primary data we store is expense records you create: amount, category, date, description, and any tags or notes you attach. This data is entirely self-reported and entered voluntarily by you.

3.4

Transaction Metadata. Each expense record carries system-generated metadata including the timestamp of creation and last modification, the record identifier, and the account to which it belongs.

3.5

Device Information. We collect information about the device you use to access the Service, including browser type and version, operating system, screen resolution, and whether you are on a mobile or desktop device.

3.6

Log and Usage Data. Our servers automatically record log data when you use the Service. This includes the pages visited, features used, time spent on each page, error logs, and the sequence of actions taken during a session.

3.7

Cookies and Similar Technologies. We use session cookies to maintain your authenticated state and preference cookies to remember settings such as date format preferences. Detailed information is provided in Section 8.

3.8

IP Address and Approximate Location. We collect your IP address for security purposes (e.g., detecting unusual login locations) and to comply with legal obligations. We do not collect precise GPS or granular location data.

3.9

Communications Data. If you contact us by email or through any support channel, we retain the contents of that correspondence, your email address, and any information you choose to include, for the purpose of responding to and resolving your enquiry.

3.10

Aggregated and Anonymised Data. We may derive aggregated, anonymised statistics from usage patterns across all users (e.g., the most common expense categories). This data cannot identify any individual and may be used for product analytics and improvement.

§ 4 How We Collect Your Information

4.1

Directly from You. Most of the data we hold is information you actively provide: when you register, when you create or edit expense records, when you update your profile, or when you contact our support team.

4.2

Automatically During Use. Technical data such as log files, device information, IP address, and session metadata are collected automatically by our servers each time you interact with the Service.

4.3

Via Cookies. Cookies placed on your browser during a session enable us to authenticate you and remember session-level preferences. You can control cookie behaviour through your browser settings; see Section 8 for details.

4.4

From Analytics Providers. We may use privacy-respecting analytics tools that collect anonymised usage data on our behalf. These tools are contractually prohibited from using your data for their own purposes.

4.5

From Hosting Infrastructure. Our hosting provider's infrastructure generates access logs that include request timestamps, HTTP status codes, and request paths as a routine part of server operation.

4.6

Inferred Data. We may infer information from your behaviour on the Service, such as inferring preferred categories based on your most frequent expense entries. Inferred data is used only to improve your in-app experience and is never shared externally.

§ 5 How We Use Your Information

5.1

Providing the Service. We use your account data and expense records to deliver the core functionality of Spendly, including displaying your transaction history, generating summaries, and enabling filtering and export.

5.2

Account Management. We use your email address to send essential transactional communications such as account verification, password reset links, and critical service notices. These messages are non-promotional and cannot be opted out of while your account remains active.

5.3

Authentication and Security. Your hashed password and session tokens are used to authenticate your identity and maintain secure sessions. IP addresses are used to detect suspicious login activity.

5.4

Personalisation. Preference data is used to personalise aspects of the Service, such as displaying your preferred currency symbol or applying your saved date format. Personalisation does not involve profiling for marketing purposes.

5.5

Product Improvement. Aggregated usage analytics help us understand which features are used most, identify pain points, and prioritise improvements. This analysis is performed on anonymised data wherever possible.

5.6

Fraud Prevention and Abuse Detection. Log data and IP information are used to identify and block abusive behaviour, spam, or attempts to compromise the security of the Service or other users' accounts.

5.7

Legal Compliance. We may process your data as required to comply with applicable laws, respond to lawful requests from government authorities, or meet regulatory obligations.

5.8

Enforcement of Our Terms. We may use data to investigate and enforce compliance with our Terms and Conditions, including investigating alleged violations and protecting our legal rights.

5.9

Support and Communications. Data from your support enquiries is used to resolve issues, improve our help documentation, and, where permitted, follow up on reported bugs or feature requests.

5.10

Research and Development. Anonymised, aggregated datasets derived from usage patterns may be used for internal research into financial behaviour trends, product feasibility studies, or academic collaboration, with strict data minimisation applied.

5.11

Promotional Communications. We will only send promotional content — such as feature announcements or newsletters — if you have explicitly consented. You may withdraw this consent at any time by clicking the unsubscribe link in any such email.

5.12

No Automated Decision-Making. We do not subject users to decisions based solely on automated processing that produce legal or similarly significant effects, without a human review step and the ability to contest the outcome.

§ 6 Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

6.1

Contractual Necessity. Processing your account data and expense records is necessary to perform the contract between you and Spendly — namely, providing the expense tracking service you signed up for.

6.2

Legitimate Interests. We process log data, device information, and aggregated analytics under our legitimate interest in maintaining the security, stability, and improvement of the Service. We have assessed that these interests do not override your fundamental rights.

6.3

Consent. Where we rely on your consent — for example, to send promotional communications — we will obtain a clear, affirmative action from you before processing begins. Consent is always optional and revocable.

6.4

Legal Obligation. Certain processing activities, such as retaining records to comply with tax or financial regulations, are carried out because we are legally required to do so under applicable law.

6.5

Vital Interests. In exceptional circumstances, we may process Personal Data to protect the vital interests of a user or another individual, such as in a genuine emergency where no other legal basis applies.

6.6

Withdrawing Consent. Where processing is based on your consent, you may withdraw that consent at any time through your account settings or by contacting us directly. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing that took place before withdrawal.

Protection of Rights. We may disclose data when we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to protect the rights, property, or safety of Spendly, our users, or the public — including to prevent fraud or address security incidents.

Aggregated Data. We may share anonymised, aggregated, non-personally identifiable data with partners, researchers, or the public for purposes such as industry reports or product benchmarking. Such data cannot be reverse-engineered to identify individuals.

With Your Explicit Consent. Outside of the scenarios described in this section, we will only share your Personal Data with third parties if you have given us specific, informed consent to do so.

Professional Advisors. We may share Personal Data with lawyers, auditors, accountants, or insurers where necessary for legitimate professional purposes, subject to professional confidentiality obligations.

§ 8 Cookies and Tracking Technologies

8.1

What Cookies Are. Cookies are small text files placed on your device by a web server when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites function efficiently and to provide session management and user preference features.

8.2

Strictly Necessary Cookies. We use session cookies that are essential for the operation of the Service — primarily to maintain your authenticated state after login. Without these cookies, the Service cannot function. They expire when you close your browser or explicitly log out.

8.3

Preference Cookies. These persistent cookies remember user preferences such as currency display, date format, and theme settings, so you do not need to re-enter them on each visit. They are retained for up to 365 days unless you clear them.

8.4

Analytics Cookies. If analytics tools are enabled, they may place their own first-party or third-party cookies to track aggregated page views and interaction events. These cookies do not store personally identifiable information.

8.5

No Advertising Cookies. We do not use advertising, retargeting, or cross-site tracking cookies. We do not participate in advertising networks or behavioural targeting programmes of any kind.

8.6

Controlling Cookies. You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to refuse new cookies, delete existing cookies, and receive a warning before a cookie is stored. Note that disabling strictly necessary cookies will prevent you from logging in to the Service.

8.7

Local Storage. In addition to cookies, we may use browser local storage to cache certain user interface state (such as the currently selected date range filter) locally on your device. This data does not leave your device and is not transmitted to our servers.

8.8

Do Not Track. Some browsers transmit a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal. At present, there is no standardised industry response to DNT signals. We do not currently alter our data collection practices based on DNT signals, but we will review this position as standards evolve.

No Responsibility for Third Parties. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of third-party services. The inclusion of links to or integrations with third-party services does not imply our endorsement of their privacy practices.

Reviewing Third-Party Policies. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party services you use in conjunction with Spendly, including your device's operating system, browser software, and internet service provider.

§ 10 Data Retention

10.1

Active Accounts. We retain your Personal Data and expense records for as long as your account remains active. Data is retained in order to provide continuity of the Service and to enable you to access your historical expense records.

10.2

After Account Deletion. When you delete your account, your expense records, profile data, and any other personally identifiable information are permanently deleted from our primary databases within 30 days of your request.

10.3

Backup Retention. Deleted data may persist in encrypted database backups for up to 90 days after deletion, after which it is irreversibly purged. Backups are not accessible to users or support staff during this period.

10.4

Legal Retention Requirements. Certain data may be retained for longer periods where required by applicable law — for example, accounting records or records related to legal disputes. In such cases, we retain only what is required and restrict access to it appropriately.

10.5

Server Log Retention. Application and server logs are automatically purged after 90 days. Anonymised log aggregates used for performance analysis may be retained indefinitely.

10.6

Requesting Early Deletion. You may request early deletion of your data at any time by deleting your account from the profile settings page or by contacting us at the address in Section 17. We will process deletion requests within 30 days.

§ 11 Data Security

11.1

Security Measures Overview. We implement reasonable and appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your Personal Data against unauthorised access, accidental loss, destruction, or damage. These measures are reviewed and updated regularly.

11.2

Encryption in Transit. All data transmitted between your browser and our servers is encrypted using TLS (Transport Layer Security). We enforce HTTPS across all Service endpoints and reject unencrypted connections.

11.3

Encryption at Rest. Sensitive fields in our database, including passwords, are stored using strong, industry-standard cryptographic hashing algorithms. Database volumes are encrypted at rest using encryption provided by our hosting infrastructure.

11.4

Access Controls. Access to production systems and user data is restricted to authorised personnel on a strict need-to-know basis. Administrative access requires multi-factor authentication and is logged for audit purposes.

11.5

Incident Response. In the event of a data breach that affects your Personal Data, we will notify affected users and relevant regulatory authorities within the timeframes required by applicable law (typically 72 hours for GDPR-covered incidents).

11.6

Limitations of Security. No method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your data, we cannot guarantee its absolute security and disclaim liability for breaches outside our reasonable control.

11.7

Reporting Vulnerabilities. If you discover a potential security vulnerability in the Service, we ask that you report it to us responsibly by contacting us directly before disclosing it publicly. We take all security reports seriously and aim to respond within 48 hours.

§ 12 Your Rights and Choices

12.1

Right to Access. You have the right to request a copy of the Personal Data we hold about you. We will provide this information in a structured, commonly used format within 30 days of a verified request.

12.2

Right to Rectification. If any information we hold about you is inaccurate or incomplete, you have the right to request that we correct or complete it. Most data can be corrected directly from your account settings.

12.3

Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"). You have the right to request the deletion of your Personal Data where it is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, where you withdraw consent, or where you object to processing and there are no overriding legitimate grounds for us to continue.

12.4

Right to Restrict Processing. In certain circumstances you may request that we restrict the processing of your Personal Data — for example, while a dispute about the accuracy of data is resolved. Data subject to a restriction will not be deleted and will not be processed further without your consent.

12.5

Right to Data Portability. Where processing is based on your consent or on a contract, you have the right to receive your Personal Data in a structured, machine-readable format (such as JSON or CSV) for transfer to another service.

12.6

Right to Object. You have the right to object at any time to the processing of your Personal Data for direct marketing purposes or where processing is based on legitimate interests. Upon receipt of a valid objection, we will cease processing unless compelling legitimate grounds exist.

12.7

Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making. You have the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing, including profiling, that produces legal or similarly significant effects concerning you.

12.8

Right to Withdraw Consent. Where we rely on your consent as the legal basis for processing, you may withdraw that consent at any time without penalty. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to withdrawal.

12.9

How to Exercise Your Rights. To exercise any of the rights listed in this section, please contact us using the details in Section 17. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling a request. We will respond without undue delay and at no charge to you.

12.10

Right to Lodge a Complaint. If you believe we have infringed your data protection rights, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the relevant supervisory authority in your country of residence. In India, this is the Data Protection Board of India. In EU/EEA countries, contact your national data protection authority.

§ 13 Children's Privacy

13.1

Minimum Age Requirement. The Service is intended for users who are at least 18 years of age, or the age of digital consent in their jurisdiction where that is higher. By registering, you represent that you meet this age requirement.

13.2

No Knowing Collection from Minors. We do not knowingly collect Personal Data from children under the age of 18. Our registration process does not solicit such information and we do not target our marketing or product design toward minors.

13.3

Parental Notification. If a parent or legal guardian becomes aware that their minor child has provided us with Personal Data without consent, they should contact us immediately using the details in Section 17.

13.4

Remediation. If we discover that we have inadvertently collected Personal Data from a minor without appropriate parental consent, we will take prompt steps to delete that data and terminate the associated account.

14.1

Where Data is Stored. Your Personal Data is primarily stored on servers located in India. Depending on your location and the infrastructure of our service providers, data may also be processed in other jurisdictions.

14.2

Cross-Border Transfers. When we transfer your Personal Data outside your country of residence — including to countries that may not have equivalent data protection standards — we take steps to ensure adequate protection is in place.

14.3

Safeguards for International Transfers. For transfers from the EU/EEA, we rely on appropriate transfer mechanisms including Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission, or the adequacy decisions where applicable.

14.4

Standard Contractual Clauses. Where Standard Contractual Clauses are used, you may request a copy of the relevant clauses by contacting us. These clauses impose equivalent data protection obligations on our overseas service providers.

14.5

Your Rights Remain Intact. Regardless of where your data is transferred or processed, you retain all the rights described in Section 12 of this policy, and we remain responsible for ensuring those rights can be exercised.

§ 15 California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

15.1

Applicability. This section applies to residents of the State of California, USA, and supplements the rest of this Privacy Policy. It is provided in accordance with the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA).

15.2

Right to Know. California residents have the right to request disclosure of the categories of Personal Data collected, the purposes for which it is used, the categories of third parties with whom it is shared, and the specific pieces of Personal Data we have collected about them in the past 12 months.

15.3

Right to Delete. California residents may request deletion of their Personal Data, subject to certain exceptions permitted by law (e.g., data needed to complete a transaction, detect security incidents, or comply with a legal obligation).

15.4

Right to Opt-Out of Sale. We do not sell Personal Data as defined under the CCPA. Therefore, there is no opt-out mechanism required. Should our practices change, we will update this policy and provide a clear opt-out mechanism before any sale occurs.

15.5

Right to Correct Inaccurate Information. California residents have the right to request that we correct inaccurate Personal Data that we maintain about them, subject to our verification of the request.

15.6

Non-Discrimination. We will not discriminate against California residents for exercising any of their CCPA rights. This means we will not deny services, charge different prices, or provide a different level of service based on the exercise of these rights.

Notification of Material Changes. For material changes — those that significantly affect your rights or how we use your data — we will notify you by email (to the address on your account) or by a prominent notice within the Service at least 14 days before the change takes effect.

16.3

Effective Date. The "Effective" date at the top of this document indicates when the current version took effect. We maintain an archive of prior versions, available on request.

16.4

Continued Use as Acceptance. Your continued use of the Service after the effective date of a revised Privacy Policy constitutes your acceptance of the changes. If you do not agree with a revised policy, you should stop using the Service and delete your account.
